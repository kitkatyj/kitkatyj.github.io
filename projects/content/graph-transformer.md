# Graph Transformer

>*"Hooray for new math, new-hoo-hoo math, it won't do you a bit of good to review math. It's so simple, so very simple, that only a child can do it!"* [Tom Lehrer, New Math, 1965](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Songs_and_Lyrics_(Lehrer)/New_Math)

I am a big fan of interactive tools being a learning material for students. When I was learning about quadratic curves in secondary school, the content was incredibly dry and I was not able to capture the essence of what I was learning. It eventually clicked onto me when I discovered that all things in physics follow a curve (the way you throw a ball in the air) and it all kinda clicked!

By playing around with the coefficients of a quadratic equation, one can make all sorts of different curves, and I wanted to create an interactive tool that can visualise that in a way that would have made me interested in learning about quadratics.

Back in my days in school, I was actually taught how to make games using HTML5 Canvas and Javascript. I wanted to expand on that, and see whether I could also make some cool interactive tools with it too. I expanded on my experience learning the ways of the canvas, and wanted to see if I can draw quadratic curves, and of course it was simple enough to do - just draw a polyline with vertices at a sufficient enough increment that it draws a smooth curve, hooray!

Having sliders on the side that automatically redraws the curve, really helps to visualise the effects of how each coefficient affects the curve. That's something as a visual learner is incredibly effective for me.

It is said that the best way to learn about the topic is to teach it, and I feel I have learned a lot more than what my school is able to offer building this! This was the first interactive tool that I built with HTML5 Canvas, and it opened the door for me to build even more crazier things.
