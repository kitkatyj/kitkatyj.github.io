 # This is Scrapbook

>“Don’t care if you think it’s cringe because it’s not your life!” Vylet Pony, Antonymph, 2021

## Childhood sacrifices and cringe
Do you remember much from your childhood? Did you have to sacrifice a part of yourself in order to grow up? Was the childlike wonder and the world we used to live in as a kid, just simply gone? 

As we grow into adults, we realise that the world begins to have different expectations of us. The friends we make as kids grow apart, we throw away parts of ourselves as they no longer resonate the same way that it does. The adult hustle changes us, it's all the growing up that really makes a mess of who we are.

Entering the working/corporate world, I realise that I can no longer meet expectations without sacrificing something in return, I begin to see my old stuff as "cringe" and hide a part of myself in fear of being judged. However, it is this feeling of cringe that really shows how far we've come, and that is something to be celebrated. 

These are the emotions I wanted to capture in a new character that will be the third of the character anthology series. A celebration of growth, cringe, and childhood whimsy.

## Seeds planted
So what object might symbolise such growth? Oh a plant! My initial character design involved a sapling in a potted plant that grows into a flower as it grows. Not just any flower, a flower that can also represent change, which was why I went with a Hydrangea, a plant that changes colour depending on the pH of the soil it is planted in. They are also known as 'Change Rose'.

However, I grew to like the idea of a book, that is able to preserve memories in it, and on top of that exploring a childhood whimsy and expression via a scrapbook. This ties into the whole "reflecting on our past selves" story, especially the idea that stickers can't easily be removed, and we would have to deal with them as part of us as we grow. 

I started discussing a character design for Scrapbook with an artist friend back in May 2022, about halfway through production of [[bismuth|This is Bismuth]], with a final design moulded and polished around October 2022.

I wanted to incorporate the ideas that were floated around for Hydrangeas, and so the Hydrangea became a part of the character design for Scrapbook as one of the stickers on his front cover.

## I am once again asking for your support
Just like This is Bismuth, I wanted a team that would be able to support in creating magic from just a crazy idea. I put out an audition video for This is Scrapbook on 12 November 2022, and revealed the character at the end, just to give a nice little tease as to what is to come to build some additional hype. Tons more applications came in compared to Bismuth's audition, and I was able to build a slightly bigger team of creatives than before including 1 songwriter, 1 musician, 3 animators, as well as 2 resident artists. We were ready to make something awesome!

## Hi there vlog!
To drive home the idea of "memories" even more, I wanted to capture Scrapbook's memories as a form of a series of vlogs, which builds a level of immersion as I wanted the viewers to be there alongside Scrapbook. This presents a unique opportunity to create a sort of [Alternate Reality Game (ARG)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternate_reality_game), and I was really excited for it. And what the heck, I wanted Scrapbook to essentially "takeover" my channel for a bit. I'm really going all out for my baby.

Multiple vlogs were planned, each covering a different stage of Scrapbook's life starting from when he receives his very first smartphone all the way to just before the music video as he heads to work, and then the eventual resolution with his friends and family. In essence, I wanted some prologue shorts to set up Scrapbooks and his friends and family, the music video will serve as the climax of the story, then followed by a series of epilogue shorts that aim to tie up the loose ends. The good ol' rising action-climax-falling action pyramid.

The first vlog got released on 1 April 2023 on April Fools' Day, and what a perfect opportunity this presents. My channel's profile picture and banner changed, and for the first couple of days I was replying to comments on the video roleplaying as Scrapbook himself. It really was such fun to become my younger self again, without restraints and just making new friends. 

Each of the early vlogs, came with its own set of custom music produced by our talented musician, who absolutely nailed the assignment. These songs were meant to parody some of the songs that were released in the late 2000s and early 2010s, which is about the timeframe when Scrapbook released the vlogs. We even got into a bit of fun and named the songs and music artist of each track to something that mimicked popular tracks. Such as "Hamper Box by Mean Day" which is a parody of Basket Case by Green Day.

## Music Video Release
5 prologue shorts were produced and released before the music video itself on 27 July 2024. I remember setting the premiere on YouTube, and I was in a call with my team as the premiere went live. It really was a magical experience, seeing the reactions live.

I am really glad we got to do those prologue shorts, as I think it provided viewers with a lot of context for the music video, which allowed them to reconnect with a character they already know and turning it into a celebration of sorts.

It was yet another massive success, and I am forever grateful to my wickedly talented team for making this happen.

## Make and Ship
One of my ambitions while being in this community was to hopefully garner enough support for my original characters that I could potentially sell them as plush toys! This is Scrapbook marks the first time this might actually be achievable, seeing the reactions from the music video, so I went ahead to look for options.

Early on, I am made aware of the company Makeship that helps creators on YouTube produce plushies. They seem to be the company that stood out amongst the rest in providing steady support and quality materials. That was the direction I set myself on after after the music video got released.

Makeship's terms was simple: in order to ensure that there's enough sales volume to produce the merch, it requires that there are at least 200 guarantees of $2 each within a 10-day petition period. Easy enough to achieve right? NOPE. The disadvantage here is that the audience I am targeting is mostly kids and teens, which aren't really known for their ability to have disposable income, much less one online. There's not a very good chance that support from the music video can be converted into sales of merchandise. But I was able to give it a shot.

To build hype, an epilogue short was published a week before, followed by the promotional video of the petition finally being launched, and another epilogue short published a week after, within the petition period. Unfortunately, the petition only achieved 157 sales after the 10-day sales window, the petition's listing eventually got taken down and is no longer accessible. Which was kinda dumb of them to do really.

However, knowing that 157 people (probably kids) were still willing to GIVE MONEY to a first time plush campaign, that's substantial support nonetheless, and I was not going to give up.

## Pass the Plushie!!
As the Makeship petition was ongoing, I was made aware of another service called objectshow.com, a service made by the object show community for the object show community. Now this was really great timing, as after the petition on Makeship did not go through I went to speak with them, and they were far more lenient on the production requirements. Plushies will still get made as part of a pre-sale, but with a much lower per unit commission if they do not meet a specific sales volume.

The plush designs were sent, and I was able to receive a sample of the eventual plush, which got me really excited. The final epilogue short for Scrapbook was hence published in October 2025, 7 months after the initial campaign from Makeship, alongside the promotional video of the plush pre-sale, which I got film together with a friend of mine!

Knowing that Scrapbook got a second chance, viewers were able to once again throw their support behind him and to bring him home. This time, during the pre-sale period of about 40 days, the plush sales exceeded the initial volume from the Makeship petition and I was incredibly excited! The Scrapbook Plush was about to be real!!

## Aftermath
Fast forward to February 2026, and I have received news that the plushies are starting to arrive at the homes of the people who have ordered it. It is honestly an indescribable feeling knowing that I have come this far, with this character concept that started way back in May 2022.

This was a journey that I like to stress, was not done by myself alone, but with a team of incredibly talented people from the community, and the support that I have received from my friends and family who have given me the opportunity to really pursue something that spoke to me, as did to everyone who watched the shorts and music video, even purchasing the plush. I feel like the luckiest person in the world.

As I stand here, writing this in end-February 2026, I look forward to the incredible things that will come as I continue to explore this wonderful life and the people I will meet along the way.

Thank you for reading until the end, you're a real one y'know?
