# This is Charcoal

## Transcending with Musical Storytelling
In 2016, my friend introduced me to the musical artist [Porter Robinson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porter_Robinson), who has just released their new single [Shelter](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fzQ6gRAEoy0). Porter has been a big influence on me and my friend who would go on the create fan edits of his songs to clips of My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic, the community in which we were both in. When Porter released his next album, [Nurture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nurture_(album)) in 2021, we were incredibly ecstatic. Porter's songs continued to inspire me. They are deep in emotion and story while also being absolute bangers. This is even more so with his songs in Nurture.

Mental health is something incredibly personal to me, having to struggle with self-doubt and self-esteem. But it is also something I see affecting my peers even more so. I think there is great meaning in storytelling that I wanted to explore myself, as nothing gets more powerful than sharing that you are not alone in facing these challenges. I am not alien to musical storytelling, as I have done quite a lot it in the My Little Pony community, notably in [[anpow|A New Pair of Wings]].

Beyond that, I believe that there is great potential in sharing these experiences with the Object Show Community, which is basically the community that spawned from the show Battle for Dream Island, which I was a part of as well. There's just an appeal with turning inanimate objects into storytelling devices, something that is still rather unseen within that community. This is how Charcoal came into being.

## Charcoal's many uses
When people think of charcoal, they typically associate it with the use as a fuel for cooking. But it is also used for other purposes such as for medicine, and for this case, as a drawing tool. I recall my first instance using charcoal to draw and was... put off by it. It stained my hands really badly and did not see myself using charcoal for drawing. But I did come to appreciate its texture both to hold and the marks it leaves behind.

This "contrast" of feelings gave me the ingredients I needed to craft Charcoal. It's meant to portray my own disdain of picking up charcoal and how it might reinforce a kind of self-inflicting critic, even though charcoal is used in so many different applications.

Continuous disapproval can be disheartening, and which can fester into self-criticism. But with friends, knowing that you're not alone, you keep going, you give it your all, sometime giving a part of yourself to really put yourself out there. 

You may even lose yourself in the process, which is what charcoal does as well, since Charcoal uses a part of themselves to produce art. That's one of the many appeals of using inanimate objects for storytelling, it's when you get to use the objects themselves as a literal part of the story. This, got me really excited to start this journey.

## Taking the first step
This was a big departure from the content that I was previously making on my YouTube channel, which mainly focused on fan work of Battle for Dream Island. Hence, why I felt symbolic that Charcoal would represent my first step into giving a part of myself into my videos. It would be the first big step I would be doing in producing my own content.

Hence, taking inspiration from my roots in the My Little Pony community, I would be taking one of Porter's new songs [Mirror](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PkiIPzG37vQ), and making a story driven music video from it, having Charcoal as the main character.

## "Character Animation"
I have never been a great character animator. Despite my [early attempts](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d0vSY15gyI0) in doing so, I am much more inclined with motion graphics instead. However, I wanted to give myself another shot using the tool I am most familiar with, After Effects. Now, After Effects CAN achieve character animation, popular My Little Pony animator [2snacks](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tK0tWD97r2Q) is known to rig their puppets within After Effects itself. However, such setups take too much time and effort, and might not necessarily work on Object Show animation.

Therefore, whatever character animation that is required to do, I tried to make it as minimal as possible. The most is just stills with the camera, walk cycles and some subtle arm movements. I think it still worked out alright.

## Release
Work on "This is Charcoal" started in March 2021, when they were introduced to my YouTube channel. The music video itself, got released on 2 July 2021. The reception was generally positive, and it gave me great confidence to work on my next project. It was genuinely heartwarming to see people in comments also sharing their own stories of self-criticism, and even bringing their own takes on the visual metaphor.

## Beyond the mark
After the music video's release, I wanted to use this opportunity to expand upon Charcoal's story, with [a comic series](https://x.com/CharcoalHeart_). This was where I got to share some more detailed world-building for Charcoal's journey before and after the events of the music video. The comic started in September 2021, and has remained in hiatus since February 2022. 

Believe me when I say I had a whole story arc planned for Charcoal, and how they tie in alongside future characters. However, as it is all too common, motivation for producing these comics faded.

However, big and better ideas beckoned, and great plans were in store for the next character, for which Charcoal marked the first step into [[bismuth|a bigger journey]].
