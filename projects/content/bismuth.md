# This is Bismuth

>"Oh, I still can't choose. Maybe it's crazy but I wish I could study a little bit of everything." Luz Noceda, The Owl House, 2020

>“Medicine, law, business, engineering, these are noble pursuits and necessary to sustain life. But poetry, beauty, romance, love, these are what we stay alive for.” [John Keating played by Robin Williams, Dead Poets Society ,1989](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aS1esgRV4Rc)

## Jack of All Trades, Master of None
Growing up, I was fascinated by many many things. I often say to my friends and family that I have "too many passions for my own good". As established in my other projects I was deeply fascinated by [[space-unicorn|astronomy]], I love [[govtech-dataviz|infographics]], [[sandbox-elements|web design]], [[apple-project|game development]], and [[charcoal|storytelling animation]].

I realise that there's just so many paths I can go down on that it becomes hard to choose exactly what I really want to do in life as a career. Dedicating time in one area, means sacrificing in another. Life appears to be set up for people going in a single direction, and everyone becomes specialists rather than generalists.

Fun fact: there would have been a parallel universe where I actually went down the path of being an animator, instead of learning computer science, if my acceptance letter went a little different. But that's a story for another time!

But to put it simply, it is a constant struggle between Art and Science. Alternatively, the Left and Right brain, Apollonian and Dionysian.

## Better than a Master of one
All of this changed when I stumbled across [this comic](https://www.facebook.com/1417241288583243/photos/a.1483896918584346/1973347926305907/) illustrated by Delusion in a Box Design, which put this all in a different perspective. There is only one path, and that is YOURS. Putting all these different disciplines is a strength that should be celebrated.

To sprinkle in some hope, people who can see differing perspectives often become leaders themselves, as they are the ones who can put themselves into the shoes of different team members who might have many different skills and experiences. This insight alone has given me new confidence in pursuing down a path of my own. This is a concept I wanted to put into a character of my own.

## Let's get down to Bismuth
I believe my first true introduction to Bismuth was in Steven Universe, with a character also named Bismuth. However, that Bismuth is a VERY different character from the Bismuth I am creating. To this day, people associate the two Bismuths, and I would like to clarify that they are different!

Anyways, I went down a little rabbit hole to understand everything that is to know about Bismuth, and felt that this was a very interesting element that deserve a bit of spotlight. Bismuth crystals on their own generate very intricate and unique geometric structures that is just incredible that they are formed naturally, which just perks both the art and science aspects very neatly. It takes science to understand the chemical bonds needed to produce the crystalline structure but also the natural love for arts to appreciate it.

I asked my sister to create designs for Bismuth in July 2021 soon after [[charcoal|This is Charcoal]] was released, finalising the design and the concepts for a music video in December. This is to be a continuation of Charcoal's music video, but no one has to have seen it to watch This is Bismuth. An anthology series of sorts, which I find the concept of pretty appealing.

## I'm putting together a team
I knew from the start, that one of the goals for This is Bismuth was to take the leap of faith and make an original song. The last vestige of derivate media that I needed to detach myself from to truly create something original. Unfortunately, I do not have much experience in songwriting, but I do have the pieces in place to make a good song. I learned the piano growing up, and understood enough music theory to pitch an idea to someone who might know what they are doing.

Also I think there's much possibility of hiring an animator to take over character animation, such that I am able to focus on producing the other aspects of the music video, for instance composition and visual effects, which I am much more confident in. I intend for these to be paid positions, as I believe that art is a privilege and having earned some advertising revenue from my YouTube channel, it's only fair to give back to the community that made me who I am today.

Hence in August 2021, I put out an audition video, seeking potential songwriters, musicians, and animators who might be able to help out in producing "This is Bismuth" with me. I eventually founded a small team of about 6 people: 1 songwriter, 2 musicians, and 3 animators, this is on top of various other artists I have commissioned to make thumbnail art and props. Hooray!

## Production and Release
About every couple weeks to a month, I would organise calls with them to check on progress, and also for me to measure my own progress as well. As poetic as it is, mixing your passions together and leading a team of incredibly creative people might just be the path for me after all.

After a year of development, and with everything set, This is Bismuth finally released on 19 August 2022 with amazing success! Reaching 100K views in 2 weeks, I think it was the fastest video of mine to reach that back then.

Similar to the reactions for Charcoal, I absolutely adore the praise that we have received, plus tons of fan art, which are honestly the best kind of praise that one can receive honestly!

With a second music video out of the way, it was time to take the next big leap. A new milestone beckoned, and I was ready for what is [[scrapbook|to come]].
