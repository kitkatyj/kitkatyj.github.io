# Inaugural GovTech National Data Viz Video Challenge

I have always been fascinated by infographics, especially animated ones. One of my biggest inspirations has been the YouTube channel [Kurzgesagt](https://www.youtube.com/@kurzgesagt). I believe I attempted to send in an application to join them once, but I never heard back from them. Oh well. Anyways, I wanted to try to delve into the space in other areas, and when an opportunity presented itself during my school years, I wanted to take it. 

Singapore's Government Technology Agency (GovTech) has organised their first ever National Data Visualisation Challenge, and their theme for this challenge was showing how we can use open data sources to solve problems in our every day. 

Our team has decided to share how we could potentially power all of Singapore with just solar power alone. Solar is the most suitable form of renewable energy available to Singapore, mostly because the other sources are not very readily available. 

I was the team's Creative Director, and got to decide how we could potentially communicate the data across. I was able to storyboard the video and relay the visual concept to my team. 

To our surprise, we got into the finals and were asked to present our findings and to a panel of judges at the Amazon offices in Singapore who was one of the sponsors of the event. To our greater surprise we actually won 1st place! 

I have always been a proponent of using renewable energy in Singapore, and I'm glad in the years since the video, major strides in solar technology have occurred, and solar farms have been slowly growing in Singapore. I think there's a lot of them perched atop of HDB flats now. I even saw a giant one plopped right next to my university when I was studying there!

The Straits Times who was at the event did an article featuring the teams [here](https://str.sg/3622).
